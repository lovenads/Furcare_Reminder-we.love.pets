Fur-tastic Health tips!

“Keep Them Healthy, Keep Them Happy.”

Balanced Diet and Proper Nutrition


Ensure your pet gets a species-appropriate, balanced diet rich in nutrients. Avoid feeding them human food that could be toxic. Trivia: Did you know that grapes and raisins can cause kidney failure in dogs, even in small amounts?

Monitor Weight and Body Condition

Regularly check your pet’s weight and avoid overfeeding. Obesity can shorten their lifespan and lead to diseases. Trivia: Just a few extra pounds in dogs or cats can be the equivalent of 20–30 pounds on a human!

Limit Treats and Table Scraps

Use treats wisely — they should be less than 10% of your pet's daily calorie intake to avoid obesity. Trivia: A small dog eating one slice of cheese is like a person eating two hamburgers — that adds up fast!

Maintain a Stress-Free Environment

Create a peaceful and safe home to reduce anxiety and stress-related behaviors in pets. Trivia: Cats can get sick from stress! Feline stress can lead to urinary tract issues and even refusal to eat.

Flea, Tick, and Worm Prevention

Use vet-approved products to prevent parasites that can cause serious health issues, like Lyme disease or heartworm. Trivia: One flea can lay up to 50 eggs per day, quickly leading to an infestation if untreated!

Understand Pet Behavior and Body Language

Learn to read your pet’s body language to better understand their emotional or physical state. Trivia: A wagging tail doesn’t always mean a dog is happy. The speed and position of the tail can indicate different emotions — like anxiety or excitement.
